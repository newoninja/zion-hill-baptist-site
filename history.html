Our Story

A Heritage of Faith in the Blue Ridge Mountains

For more than a century, Zion Hill Baptist Church has stood as a local lighthouse in the mountains.

Our Story

In the spring of 1914, in the quiet community then known as Splendor, North Carolina, a small group of believers gathered under a canvas tent to worship. They had no building, but they had a profound hunger for the Word of God and a vision for a local lighthouse in the mountains.

On May 24, 1916, that vision took permanent shape as Zion Hill Baptist Church moved into its first building. For over a century, while the world around us has changed, the message preached within these walls has remained the same.

Today, Zion Hill stands as a traditional, independent Baptist church dedicated to the old-time way. We believe in the power of congregational singing, the warmth of Christian fellowship, and the life-changing authority of the King James Bible. Whether you are a lifelong resident of Henderson County or just passing through the beautiful Zirconia area, our doors and our hearts are open to you.

Milestones

1914: Tent Revival

A small body of believers began meeting in worship under a canvas tent in the mountain community of Splendor.

May 24, 1916: First Building

The church moved into its first permanent building, establishing Zion Hill as a lasting witness in the area.

Today: Continuing the Old-Time Way

Zion Hill remains committed to traditional worship, biblical preaching, and Christ-centered fellowship.
